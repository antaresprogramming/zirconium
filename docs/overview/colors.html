Colors

The set of colors used in Zirconium.

Francis Rubio
July 19, 2021
Zirconium

Most of the colors used by Zirconium comes from the elementary OS color palette. This is to reduce time in formulating and testing colors that work for aesthetics, usability, and accessibility. The following diagrams will show the colors used throughout Zirconium.

Brand colors

Zirconium uses Banana as its brand color. It is a specific shade of yellow used by the elementary OS color palette. We also use this as the warning color for some controls.

Accent colors

Zirconium uses Blueberry as its accent color. It is a specific shade of blue used by the elementary OS color palette. Neutral Colors

Zirconium uses Black and Silver as its neutral colors. They are both used as foreground and background colors. In light mode (default), Black is used for the foreground, and Silver is used for the background. In dark mode, these are reversed, and Silver becomes the foreground, while Black is used for the background.

Both of these colors currently have three levels each. These levels are used to signify elevation and difference in context.

Dark mode

One of the goals of Zirconium is to support dark mode by default. This is achieved by providing custom styling using the prefers-color-scheme: dark media query.

Although Zirconium does not make dark or light theme available on demand (perhaps via a switch control that some websites use), Zirconium responds to your device's system setting on whether to use dark mode or not.
